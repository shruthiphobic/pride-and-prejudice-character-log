Chapter 1

  • Desperate to get one of her daughters married to Mr. Bingley.
  • “When a woman has five grown-up daughters, she ought to give over thinking of her own beauty.”
  • Favors Lydia and Jane over Lizzy.
  • Still can’t understand what her husband’s like.
  • "Her mind was less difficult to develop."
  • “She was a woman of mean understanding, little information, and uncertain temper.”
  • “When she was discontented, she fancied herself nervous.”
  • “The business of her life was to get her daughters married; its solace was visiting and news.”

Chapter 2

  • Switches up on Mrs. Long despite being happy that she told her about Mr. Bingley in the previous chapter.
  • She’s the type to take it out on her daughters when she gets heated.
  • Views other women as competition (Mrs. Long).
  • Surprised and starts praising Mr. Bennet once he tells her that he did visit Mr. Bingley.
  • Was really happy that her husband did pay Mr. Bingley a visit.

Chapter 3

  • Kept pestering her husband with questions about Mr. Bingley.
  • Worried that Mr. Bingley would travel around all the time when he couldn’t accept their invitation to dinner.
  • Absolutely hated Mr. Darcy because he insulted Elizabeth.
