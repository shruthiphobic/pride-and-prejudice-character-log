Chapter 4

  • "Their manners are not equal to his," as said by Elizabeth about the sisters.
  • Jane didn't like them at first but then she got to know them and thinks they're "pleasing women."
  • "I am much mistaken if we shall not find a very charming neighbor in her," as said by Jane about Miss Bingley.
  • "their behavior at the assembly had not been calculated to please in general."
  • "They were very fine ladies, not deficient in good humor when they were pleased, nor in the power of making themselves agreeable when they chose it, but proud and conceited."
  • She and her sister were pretty, well educated, had a fortune of 20k per year, spent more than they wanted to, and looked at people based on rank.
  • They thought highly of themselves but were mean towards others.
  • "They were of a respectable family in the north of England; a circumstance more deeply impressed on their memories than that their brother's fortune and their own had been acquired by trade."
  • Was anxious that Mr. Bingley had a house of his own but was willing to stay with him.
  • Wanted to get to know Jane more.
