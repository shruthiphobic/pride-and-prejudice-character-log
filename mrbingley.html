Chapter 1

  • “Young man of large fortune from the north of England.”
  • A single man with a fortune of 4k/5k a year.
  • Possibly seeking a wife.

Chapter 3

  • “quite young, wonderfully handsome, extremely agreeable.”
  • Likes dancing.
  • Wanted to see the daughters when he visited Longbourne.
  • Wore a blue coat and rode a black horse.
  • “good looking and gentlemanlike; he had a pleasant countenance and easy, unaffected manners.”
  • “lively and unreserved, danced every dance, was angry that the ball closed so early, and talked of giving one himself at Netherfield.”
  • Basically, the opposite of Mr. Darcy since he was so sociable and friendly towards everyone.
  • Tries to get Mr. Darcy to dance with Elizabeth.
  • Really likes Jane and thinks she’s really pretty.

Chapter 4

  • “sensible, good humored, lively; and I never saw such happy manners! -so much ease, such perfect good breeding,” as said by Jane.
  • Inherited his dad's property worth 100k pounds.
  • It was unknown whether or not he'd stay at Netherfield for the rest of his life and leave it for future generations.
  • Made the decision to buy Netherfield really quickly since he liked what he saw almost immediately.
  • Liked Darcy because of the "easiness, openness, and ductility of his temper," even though they were completely different.
  • Bingley wasn't stupid but Darcy was just clever.
  • He knew he'd be liked wherever he went in contrast to Darcy.
  • He thought all the people at the Meryton assembly were pleasant since everyone was kind and attentive to him. He easily became friends with everyone.
  • He didn't think anyone was more prettier than Jane.

Chapter 6

  • Obviously in love with Jane, shows it pretty well.
