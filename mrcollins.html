Chapter 13

  • Slated to inherit Mr. Bennet's property once he dies and can kick the others out as he pleases once he inherits it.
  • Described as "odious" by Mrs. Bennet.
  • His father quarreled with Mr. Bennet a lot.
  • Wanted to end the feud between his father and Mr. Bennet but held back because he felt that he would be disrespectful to be on good terms with Mr. Bennet.
  • Funded by Lady Catherine de Bourgh.
  • He's a clergyman who wants to "promote and establish the blessing of peace in all families within the reach of [his] influence."
  • He claims that he would make any amends with his daughters.
  • "He seems to be a most conscientious and polite young man, upon my word, and I doubt not will prove a valuable acquaintance," as said by Mr. Bennet.
  • Elizabeth was surprised by his "deference for Lady Catherine, and his kind intention of christening, marrying, and burying his parishioners whenever it were required."
  • Elizabeth feels that he is pompous.
  • "There is a mixture of servility and self-importance in his letter."
  • Was punctual and received with politeness.
  • Was willing to talk to the daughters.
  • "tall, heavy looking young man of five and twenty. His hair was grave and stately, and his manners were very formal."
  • Compliments the daughters.

Chapter 14

  • Really passionate about Lady Catherine de Bourgh.
  • Likes complimenting women and "flattering with delicacy."
  • He compliments based on what he observes in the present and not through previous observations.
  • Mr. Bennet thinks he's absurd.
  • Extremely boring when he reads books.
  • Got offended by Lydia interrupting him and stopped reading.
