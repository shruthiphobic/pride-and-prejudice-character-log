Chapter 1

  • Enjoys teasing his wife.
  • Tends to favor Lizzy over the other daughters. He thinks they’re foolish.
  • “I have a high respect for your nerves. They are my old friends. I have heard you mention them with consideration these last twenty years at least.”
  • “was so odd a mixture of quick parts, sarcastic humor, reserve, and caprice, that the experience of three-and-twenty years had been insufficient to make his wife understand his character.”

Chapter 2

  • Always intended on visiting Mr. Bingley even though he kept teasing his wife by saying he wouldn’t go.
  • Wanted to surprise his family by telling them that he visited Mr. Bingley when they thought that he was yet to meet their family.
  • “he left the room, fatigued with the raptures of his wife.”

Chapter 3

  • Doesn’t really give into constant pestering (his wife and kids asking him about Mr. Bingley).
  • Hoped that his wife would be disappointed with Mr. Bingley (presumably to get her to stop going on and on about him).
  • Tired of his wife going on and on about who Mr. Bingley danced with.
