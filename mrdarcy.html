Chapter 3

  • “drew the attention of the room by his fine, tall person, handsome features, noble mein.”
  • Made 10k a year.
  • “much handsomer than Mr. Bingley, and he was looked at with great admiration for about half the evening, till his manners gave a disgust which turned the tide of his popularity.”
  • “discovered to the proud, to be above his company, and above being pleased.”
  • “forbidding, disagreeable countenance, and being unworthy to be compared with his friend.”
  • Was mostly standoffish during the night and only danced with the women he came with.
  • “proudest, most disagreeable man in the world, and everybody hoped that he would never come there again.”
  • Hates dancing unless he knows his partner well.
  • Calls Elizabeth "tolerable."
  • “disagreeable, horrid man, not at all worth pleasing. So high and so conceited that there was no enduring him,” as described by Mrs. Bennet.

Chapter 4

  • He likes Bingley because he had the "firmest reliance, and of his judgement the highest opinion."
  • "In understanding, Darcy was the superior."
  • He's clever.
  • Darcy was arrogant, reserved, and nitpicky. His manners weren't really inviting either.
  • He seemed to offend people wherever he went.
  • He saw "little beauty and no fashion" in the people at the Meryton assembly and wasn't interested in talking to anyone.
  • Nobody gave him attention or pleasure.
  • He thought Jane was pretty but she smiled too much (nitpicky).
